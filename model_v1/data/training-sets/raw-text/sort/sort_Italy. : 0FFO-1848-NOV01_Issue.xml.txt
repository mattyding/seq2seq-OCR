 The Augsburg Gazette has letters from Trieste to the 20th ult
, stating that the whole of the German fleet, with the exception of two steamers cruising in the Venetian waters, had retired to Pirano on account of the sirocco storms; they were accompanied by the English and French ships
 It was thought that Admiral Albini would soon return to Trieste
 Tranquillity was undisturbed in that city, but trade was at a stand
 The Austrian frigate Bellona and the French steam-frigate Asmodee arrived at Trieste on the 19th
 The utmost anxiety for accounts from Vienna was generally felt
